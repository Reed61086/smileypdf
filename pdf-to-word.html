Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the PDF to Word conversion?

Our converter maintains basic text content and simple formatting. Complex layouts, special fonts, or intricate design elements may not convert perfectly and might require some manual adjustments. The accuracy depends on the complexity and structure of your original PDF.

Can I convert password-protected PDFs?

Currently, our tool cannot process password-protected or encrypted PDFs. You'll need to remove the protection before uploading. If you have the password, you can remove the protection using Adobe Acrobat or other PDF editing software before using our converter.

What's the difference between DOC and DOCX formats?

DOC is the older Microsoft Word format, while DOCX is the newer format introduced in Word 2007. DOCX files are generally smaller, more secure, and offer better compatibility with modern word processors. Our tool converts PDF to the DOCX format, which provides better formatting preservation and compatibility with current versions of Microsoft Word.

Is there a file size limit?

While our tool can handle most PDF files, browser limitations may affect performance with extremely large documents (over 100MB). For best results, we recommend files under 50MB. If you have a larger file, consider splitting it into smaller sections before conversion.

Is my data secure when using this converter?

Yes, your data security is our priority. Our PDF to Word converter processes files entirely in your browser - your files are never uploaded to any server. This client-side processing ensures complete privacy and security for your sensitive documents.

Can I convert multiple PDFs at once?

Currently, our tool processes one PDF file at a time. For batch conversion of multiple files, you'll need to convert each document individually. We're working on adding batch conversion capabilities in a future update.
