Guide: Converting Images (JPG, PNG, WEBP) to PDF

Published on June 15, 2024

{/* Placeholder Date */}

Need to combine photos, screenshots, or other images into a single PDF document? Maybe you're creating a portfolio, archiving receipts, or preparing a visual report. Converting multiple images into one PDF makes them easier to share, print, and manage.

Why Convert Images to PDF?

  • Standardization: PDF is a universally accepted format, ensuring recipients can easily open and view the file.
  • Sharing: Sending one PDF is simpler than sending multiple image files.
  • Printing: PDFs offer better control over page layout, size, and margins for printing compared to individual images.
  • Organization: Keep related images grouped together in a single, multi-page document.

How to Use an Online Image to PDF Converter

Using a tool like SmileyPDF's Image to PDF Converter simplifies the process:

  1. Upload Images: Go to the Image to PDF tool. Drag and drop your JPG, PNG, or WEBP files, or click "Select Images" to browse. You can upload multiple images at once.
  2. Order Your Images: Previews of your uploaded images will appear. Drag and drop these previews to set the order you want the images to appear as pages in the PDF. Use the button to remove individual images.
  3. Customize Settings: Before converting, adjust the options:

    • Page Orientation: Choose 'Portrait' (taller) or 'Landscape' (wider).
    • Page Size: Select standard sizes like A4, US Letter, or Legal.
    • Margin: Set the white space (in mm) around each image on its PDF page (e.g., 10mm is common).
  4. Generate PDF: Click the "Generate PDF" button. The tool will create the PDF document containing your images, one per page, according to your settings.
  5. Download: A download button will appear. Click it to save your newly created PDF file. You can usually preview it first.

Tips for Best Results

  • Image Quality: Start with the highest quality images possible for the best PDF output. Very low-resolution images may look pixelated in the PDF.
  • File Order: Check the image order in the preview section carefully before generating the PDF.
  • Margins: Adding a small margin (e.g., 5-10mm) often looks more professional and prevents content from being cut off when printing.
  • Orientation: Choose the orientation that best fits the majority of your images (e.g., portrait for most photos, landscape for wide graphics). The tool will scale images to fit within the chosen page orientation and margins.

Converting images to PDF is a breeze with free online tools. It's a great way to package and share visual content professionally and securely, directly from your browser.
